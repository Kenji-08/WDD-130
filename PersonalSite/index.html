Japanese Dishes

Nigiri

Hand-pressed sushi: a slice of raw fish or seafood placed atop a small ball of vinegared rice. Common toppings include salmon, tuna, or shrimp.

Maki

Rolled sushi wrapped in seaweed (nori), cut into bite-sized pieces. Filled with ingredients like cucumber, avocado, and fish. California rolls and tuna rolls are examples.

Uramaki

Inside-out rolls where the rice is on the outside and nori is inside. Often topped or coated with sesame seeds, fish roe, or sauces.

Temaki

Hand-rolled cones of nori filled with rice, vegetables, and fish. Eaten like a sushi ice cream cone, great for casual settings.

Inari

Sushi rice stuffed inside sweet, marinated tofu pouches. Vegetarian-friendly and mildly sweet.

Shoyu Ramen

Soy sauce-based broth—clear, brown, and savory with a slightly tangy, salty flavor. Often served with chicken, bamboo shoots, and nori.

Miso Ramen

Rich, hearty broth made from fermented soybean paste. Originating in Hokkaido, it’s thick and nutty, often topped with corn, butter, pork, and bean sprouts.

Shio Ramen

Salt-based broth—light, clear, and often made with seafood or chicken. Tends to be more delicate and less fatty, with a clean flavor profile.

Tonkotsu Ramen

Creamy, rich broth made by boiling pork bones for hours. It’s thick, cloudy, and savory, with a deep umami taste. Popular in Kyushu.

Best Restaurants to Visit

Sukiyabashi Jiro
Sushi
Jiro Ono’s legendary 10-seat sushi bar in Ginza has become iconic worldwide, especially after Jiro Dreams of Sushi. Specializing in Edomae-style sushi, the experience is fast-paced and elegant, with each piece masterfully crafted and served in quick succession. Due to high demand, reservations are only accepted through hotel concierges. Expect quiet, focused dining and an emphasis on rice texture and temperature control.
Signature dish: Maguro Nigiri
Sushi Saito
Sushi
This 3-Michelin-star sushi haven in Roppongi is helmed by Takashi Saito, famous for his precision and approachability. Although currently invitation-only, it was once Tokyo’s top sushi reservation. The atmosphere is intimate (only 8 seats), and the ingredients are aged, marinated, or lightly torched to highlight their best flavors.
Signature dish: Otoro (Fatty Tuna)
Takagaki no Sushi
Sushi
Tucked in Nihonbashi, this Michelin-starred gem is known for traditional Edomae-style sushi in a serene Taisho-era home. The experience blends seasonal seafood, simplicity, and impeccable hospitality. A great balance of prestige and approachability compared to the more exclusive giants.
Signature dish: Kohada (Gizzard Shad)
Ippudo
Ramen
One of Japan’s most successful ramen chains, Ippudo originated in Fukuoka and is known for smooth, creamy tonkotsu broth with thin Hakata-style noodles. Shops are stylish, staff are friendly, and bowls can be customized. Their global popularity doesn’t detract from the quality you’ll find in Japanese locations.
Signature dish: Shiromaru Classic
Ichiran
Ramen
Designed for solo ramen lovers, Ichiran features individual dining booths and a complete customization system for spice, richness, and noodle texture. You order via ticket machine, and the experience is focused on savoring every detail of the tonkotsu broth.
Signature dish: Classic Tonkotsu Ramen
Menya Sho Honten
Ramen
A standout in the competitive Shinjuku ramen scene, Menya Sho specializes in elevated shio (salt) ramen. Their broth is a fusion of chicken, seafood, and dried ingredients, producing a clean yet layered taste. Often featured in ramen guides and lists.
Signature dish: Shio Ramen
Oshima
Ramen
Inspired by Sapporo’s famed Sumire ramen, Oshima offers hearty, stir-fried miso ramen that’s perfect for colder seasons. The rich, oily broth and caramelized vegetables give it a deep, toasty flavor rarely matched outside Hokkaido.
Signature dish: Miso Ramen
